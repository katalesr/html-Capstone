Genius: An intellectual power of a sublime type, such as that attributed to those
who are considered the greatest practitioners in any branch of art, meditation, or application.
Terman and Hollingworth used the term genius to denote children with high intelligence,
as Terman considered each student of the sample that he studied and followed up
about 35 years, got +140 IQ points in the Stanford-Binet test among geniuses.

Talent: complex features that qualify the individual for high achievement in some
skills and jobs, and the gifted is the individual who has an innate aptitude
and is cultivated by the appropriate environment, so talent often
appears in a specific field such as music, poetry, drawing, or even quick intuition... and others.

Creativity: the production of new, rare, different, useful thought or action, and thus depends on tangible achievement.

Intelligence: the general overall ability to perform intentional action, think rationally, and interact adequately with the
environment. Intelligence is the capabilities of the individual in several areas, such as high abilities in
vocabulary and numbers, concepts and problem-solving, the ability to benefit from experiences, and learn new information.
